ADAPTIVE TEMPORAL-STRUCTURAL FUSION MODEL FOR MINERAL COMMODITY DEMAND FORECASTING
________________________________________________________________________________

                              Chapter 5
                         Analysis and Design

This chapter describes the comprehensive analysis and design of the Adaptive
Temporal-Structural Fusion (ATSF) system. It details the system's software and
hardware requirements, outlines the high-level system architecture and data flow,
presents the statistical characteristics of the two primary datasets utilised
for evaluation, and examines the graph construction methodology governing the
relational topology of both the SupplyGraph and USGS networks.

5.2 SYSTEM BLOCK DIAGRAM
________________________________________________________________________________

The ATSF system block diagram (Figure 5.1) illustrates the parallel processing
architecture of the model. The architecture is constructed to ensure that
temporal and structural signals are evaluated independently before fusion,
allowing the adaptive layer to diagnose their relative predictive utility.

The sequence operates as follows:
(1) Data Inputs & Preprocessing: Raw SupplyGraph CSV and USGS CSV files are
ingested, sequentially normalised, and converted into structured PyTorch [22]
tensors.
(2) Graph Construction: The parsed data is structured into PyTorch Geometric [23]
Data objects encoding multi-relational adjacencies.
(3) Parallel Branch Processing:
    - TFT Branch (src/models/tft_branch.py): Extracts sequential patterns via
      a Variable Selection Network, LSTM, and multi-head self-attention.
    - GAT Branch (src/models/gat_branch.py): Aggregates structural
      intercommodity signals via typed attention gates.
(4) Adaptive Fusion Layer (src/models/fusion_layer.py): Combines the two branch
embeddings using the learned scalar alpha.
(5) Demand Output Head: Generates deterministic point forecasts and probabilistic
quantile predictions.
(6) Risk Scoring Interface: Translates the mathematical outputs into actionable
supply-chain risk assessments (budget stress, lead-time risk, criticality).

5.3 SYSTEM FLOW CHART
________________________________________________________________________________

The end-to-end system flow is orchestrated across nine sequential pipeline
stages, bridging raw data acquisition to final statistical results.

1. Raw Data (data/raw/): Persistent storage of unmodified SupplyGraph and
   USGS textual and tabular sources.
2. Preprocessing: Handled by supplygraph_loader.py, usgs_loader.py,
   and usgs_preprocessing.py, converting textual data into zero-inflated
   or temporally sparse tensors.
3. Graph Construction: Assembled by graph_builder.py, parsing edge lists
   into heterogeneous typed graph topologies.
4. Training: Orchestrated by trainer.py (via the train.py entry point),
   applying the multi-quantile pinball loss and boundary regularisation.
5. Multi-seed Runs: Automated by run_multi_seed.py across five distinct
   seeds (42, 123, 456, 789, 1337) for rigorous reproducibility.
6. Baseline Comparison: Evaluated by run_classical_baselines.py (ARIMA, XGBoost)
   and run_graph_baselines.py (TFT-only, GAT-only, Fixed Fusion ablations).
7. Evaluation: Processed by evaluate.py, applying the trained models to
   the strictly chronological held-out test sets.
8. Risk Scoring: Executed by demo_risk_scoring.py, mapping tensor outputs
   to categorical business logic thresholds.
9. Results: Written to the results/ directory, capturing JSON logs,
   metrics CSVs, and generated trajectory figures.

5.5 GRAPH CONSTRUCTION DESIGN
________________________________________________________________________________

The graph construction process, handled by src/data/graph_builder.py, uniquely
structures each dataset's topological inter-dependencies using categorically
typed edges.

5.5.1 USGS MINERAL COMMODITY GRAPH:
The USGS network connects 127 mineral commodity nodes using 15 distinct,
causally expressive edge types. These 15 typed relations represent physical,
geopolitical, and economic linkages extracted from the USGS Mineral Commodity
Summaries:
(1) technology_cluster, (2) alloy_components, (3) construction_coproduction,
(4) electronics_coproduction, (5) substitution, (6) battery_coproduction,
(7) critical_mineral_designation, (8) geopolitical_supply_risk,
(9) price_correlation, (10) recycling_secondary_source,
(11) byproduct_coproduction, (12) catalyst_role, (13) refractory_industrial,
(14) functional_coating, and (15) supply_chain_input.
This rich semantic variety provides the GAT branch with robust structural
context capable of partially compensating for the sparse 3-4 year temporal
training window.

5.5.2 SUPPLYGRAPH TAXONOMIC GRAPH:
The SupplyGraph network connects 40 nodes via 4 structural edge types:
product_group, sub_group, plant, and storage_location. Unlike the USGS graph,
SupplyGraph's four edge types encode taxonomic product classification, not
causal demand dependencies. They indicate product co-membership in
organisational categories rather than direct supply-chain substitution or
co-production relationships. This structural shallowness is reflected in
the model's converged alpha approximation of 0.919, demonstrating that the
ATSF mechanism correctly identifies the relative informational weakness of
taxonomic links and autonomously routes prediction emphasis back to the
temporal branches.
