ADAPTIVE TEMPORAL-STRUCTURAL FUSION MODEL FOR MINERAL COMMODITY DEMAND FORECASTING
________________________________________________________________________________

                              Chapter 9
                              Conclusion

The Adaptive Temporal-Structural Fusion (ATSF) model developed and evaluated
in this project successfully integrates temporal sequence modelling through the
TFT branch, structural inter-commodity reasoning through the multi-relational
GAT branch, and a context-conditioned adaptive scalar fusion mechanism (alpha),
achieving a USGS WAPE of 7.01%+-0.83% — with a 95% confidence interval
[5.99%, 8.03%] entirely below the deterministic XGBoost baseline of 8.07% —
and a SupplyGraph WAPE of 55.07%+-1.94%, representing a 30.1% reduction over
the best neural alternative, across five independently seeded experimental runs.
The learned fusion weight alpha converges to 0.919+-0.010 on daily SupplyGraph
data (near-complete temporal dominance) and oscillates near 0.489+-0.197 on
annual USGS data (balanced mixed regime), quantifying a 43.0 percentage-point
structural contribution gap between the two datasets that constitutes the core
empirical finding of this project. An interactive Streamlit-based agentic web
platform further delivers the model's structural insights as plain-language
procurement intelligence, bridging the gap between complex graph neural network
outputs and non-technical supply-chain stakeholders.

9.1 CONTRIBUTIONS TO THE FIELD
________________________________________________________________________________

This project advances the state of the art in multi-modal demand forecasting
for mineral commodity supply chains through five primary contributions.

1. CONTEXT-CONDITIONED ADAPTIVE FUSION MECHANISM:

A per-instance scalar fusion weight alpha in (0, 1) is learned end-to-end,
conditioned on material type and forecast horizon, replacing fixed-weight
blending and entropy regularisation with one-sided boundary regularisation
(Equation 3). This permits strong unimodal preference — such as the alpha=0.919
observed on SupplyGraph — when empirically supported by the training data,
whilst maintaining gradient flow toward balanced fusion when structural signal
is informative. The mechanism replaces a subjective design assumption (what
fixed weight to use) with an empirically learned, per-instance diagnostic.

2. EMPIRICAL DEMONSTRATION OF DATASET-DEPENDENT MODALITY PREFERENCE:

Through five independently seeded experiments on two structurally distinct
datasets, the project produces the first quantitative measurement of the
dataset-dependence of temporal versus structural modality importance. Alpha
converges to 0.919+-0.010 on daily SupplyGraph (temporal dominance; 8.1%
structural contribution) and to 0.489+-0.197 on annual USGS data (mixed regime;
51.1% structural contribution) — a 43.0 percentage-point structural contribution
gap. The per-category alpha standard deviation of 0.021 on SupplyGraph versus
0.215 on USGS further confirms that modality preference is a property of the
dataset's structural characteristics, not a hyperparameter artifact.

3. SUPERIOR PERFORMANCE OVER ALL FIXED-WEIGHT NEURAL ALTERNATIVES:

ATSF achieves a 30.1% WAPE reduction over the best neural alternative on
SupplyGraph (GAT-only at 78.77%+-5.18%) and a USGS WAPE of 7.01%+-0.83%
with a 95% confidence interval [5.99%, 8.03%] entirely below the strongest
classical baseline (XGBoost at 8.07%). Additionally, Fixed Fusion —
notwithstanding its competitive mean WAPE on USGS — exhibits 5x higher variance
(+-5.17% versus ATSF's +-0.83%), demonstrating that a fixed ratio cannot
reliably satisfy planning requirements across independently initialised runs.
The Wilcoxon signed-rank test [26] yields statistic=0.0 and p=0.0625 for all
SupplyGraph comparisons, the minimum achievable for n=5, confirming strong
empirical consistency across seeds.

4. PROBABILISTIC UNCERTAINTY QUANTIFICATION AND RISK INTERFACE:

The TFT quantile heads produce calibrated P10-P90 prediction intervals achieving
79.1%+-4.0% coverage on USGS (target 80%) and 84.5%+-5.96% on SupplyGraph,
demonstrating interval calibration through the multi-quantile pinball loss
[14][15]. The Layer 5 rule-based risk scoring interface translates forecasts and
GAT attention weights into actionable procurement risk assessments across budget
stress, lead-time risk, and dependency criticality dimensions. This layer is
disclosed as a proof-of-concept with synthetic lead-time proxies and is not
validated against real procurement outcomes [Limitation 4].

5. AGENTIC PROCUREMENT INTELLIGENCE PLATFORM:

The Streamlit-based interactive web platform delivers the empirical findings of
the ATSF model — structural ablation metrics, per-seed alpha values, edge
attention weights — as natural-language procurement intelligence through a
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipeline powered by the
meta/llama-3.1-70b-instruct model. The dual-LLM orchestration pipeline, native
ATSF subgraph extraction from all_edges.csv, and the physics-based PyVis graph
engine together transform complex graph neural network outputs into accessible
decision-support tools for practitioners without machine learning expertise.

9.2 LIMITATIONS AND CONSTRAINTS
________________________________________________________________________________

Despite its strong performance, the ATSF model is subject to the following
seven limitations, all of which must be acknowledged in any deployment or
extension of this work.

1. LOW-DATA REGIME: The USGS dataset contains only five annual observations
per mineral commodity node; only 3-4 observations are used for training under
the rolling-window cross-validation scheme. External validation on longer
time series — as more annual USGS data becomes available — is required to
confirm the generalisability of the reported results.

2. SAMPLE SIZE: The small sample of n=5 seeds limits the statistical
significance of the reported results; the minimum achievable Wilcoxon
signed-rank p-value for n=5 is 0.0625. We report strong empirical consistency
across seeds as the primary claim of superiority, acknowledging that
conventional significance thresholds cannot be satisfied with this seed count.

3. TEMPORAL BIAS: The 222-day SupplyGraph evaluation window biases the test
set predominantly toward late-period EEA product group behaviour, as
chronological splitting concentrates EEA-group products in the final test
partition. Performance on other product groups and time periods may differ.

4. SYNTHETIC PROXIES: Lead-time values in Layer 5 are synthetically assigned
(group-level constants from [3, 30] days for SupplyGraph; fixed 1.0 for USGS).
The risk interface is a proof-of-concept demonstration and has not been
validated against real logistics or procurement outcome data.

5. COMPUTE SCALABILITY: All experiments were conducted on a single NVIDIA T4
GPU on GCP Vertex AI Workbench. Scalability of the training and inference
pipeline to larger supply-chain graphs — with more nodes, more edge types,
or longer temporal windows — has not been evaluated.

6. STATIC GRAPH TOPOLOGY: The supply-chain graph structure used in both
datasets is static: edge types and confidence weights are fixed across the
entire training and evaluation period. Dynamic edge modelling — where
structural relationships evolve as geopolitical, technological, or market
conditions change — remains future work.

7. GENERALISATION SCOPE: The model's performance has been validated on exactly
two datasets (SupplyGraph and USGS). Its accuracy, calibration, and modality
preference behaviour in other industrial domains, other commodity classes,
or other temporal resolutions remain to be empirically validated.

________________________________________________________________________________
9.3 FUTURE WORK DIRECTIONS
________________________________________________________________________________

Future research will focus on three primary directions that directly address
the limitations identified above.

1. DYNAMIC GRAPH MODELLING: Developing multi-relational supply-chain graphs
with learnable, time-varying edge weights would allow the GAT branch to capture
the evolution of structural dependencies under changing geopolitical or
technological conditions. This would eliminate Limitation 6 and enable the
model to detect structural regime changes — for instance, the emergence of
new substitution pathways when a critical mineral becomes geopolitically
restricted — that are invisible to the current static graph formulation.

2. EXTENDED USGS TIME SERIES: As additional annual observations from the USGS
Mineral Commodity Summaries become available in future years, evaluating the
adaptive fusion mechanism on longer USGS time series will directly address
Limitation 1. With a 10-year window, the ATSF model's alpha behaviour could
be validated across multiple commodity super-cycles, providing stronger evidence
for the dataset-dependence of modality preference across genuinely distinct
demand regimes.

3. OPERATIONAL VALIDATION OF RISK SCORING: Conducting prospective validation
studies of the Layer 5 risk scoring interface against actual procurement and
logistics records — real lead times, real budget stress events, and real
supplier disruptions — would transform the proof-of-concept demonstration into
an operationally validated decision-support tool. This would require partnership
with a supply-chain operator willing to provide historical procurement outcome
data for calibration and back-testing of the rule-based thresholds.

9.4 CLOSING REMARKS
________________________________________________________________________________

The ATSF model demonstrates that the balance between temporal dynamics and
structural relational signal is a fundamental property of the dataset rather
than a universal constant to be assumed by the model designer. The learnable
scalar alpha serves not merely as a tuning parameter but as a dataset-level
structural diagnostic — quantifying, in real time and without additional probing,
the degree to which a given dataset's supply-chain graph encodes genuinely
causal inter-commodity dependencies as opposed to shallow taxonomic
classification. The 43.0 percentage-point difference in structural contribution
between SupplyGraph (8.1%) and USGS (51.1%) — emerging autonomously from
training without any dataset-specific prior — provides a compelling empirical
argument that adaptive fusion should be the default design choice for
multi-modal spatio-temporal forecasting systems applied across structurally
heterogeneous domains.

By providing both accurate probabilistic forecasts and human-interpretable
evidence for those forecasts — the alpha value as a modality diagnostic, the
edge ablation ranking as a structural audit, and the Grad-CAM-equivalent GAT
attention map as a relational heatmap — the ATSF system offers a robust
framework for resilient industrial intelligence that is simultaneously
verifiable by domain experts and actionable by procurement practitioners.
The agentic Streamlit platform further ensures that these technical insights
are not confined to research environments but are accessible to the supply-chain
stakeholders whose decisions they are designed to inform. This project is one
contribution toward the goal of AI-enabled, trustworthy, and transparent
industrial forecasting infrastructure for the critical minerals that underpin
global clean energy transition.
